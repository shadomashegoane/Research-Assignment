Course Overview
This assignment is designed for beginner students to build a strong foundational understanding of the data landscape
. It covers key concepts ranging from traditional data storage to advanced artificial intelligence and developer tools
.
Assignment Structure
The research is divided into five core sections:
Section A: Data Fundamentals – Covers databases, data warehouses, data lakes, data modeling, ETL processes, and various data storage formats
.
Section B: Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning – Explores the differences between AI, ML, and Deep Learning, as well as specific technologies like NLP, LLMs, and Generative AI
.
Section C: APIs, Tools & Developer Concepts – Focuses on technical essentials such as REST APIs, JSON, version control with Git, Jupyter Notebooks, and Python libraries
.
Section D: Data Ethics, Governance & Careers – Examines data privacy (GDPR), ethics, AI bias, and the various roles within the data professional field
.
Section E: Personal Reflection – A concluding section for students to reflect on the concepts they found most interesting or challenging
.
Submission Instructions
To successfully complete and submit this assignment, students must adhere to the following guidelines:
Handwritten Only: All answers must be handwritten; typed submissions will not be accepted
.
Original Work: Answers must be written in full sentences and in the student's own words based on their research
.
Bonus Marks: Including diagrams or relevant examples in the answers will earn bonus marks
.
Citations: Sources must be cited at the bottom of each section
. A final reference list should also be provided at the end of the assignment
.
Submission Method: Completed work must be scanned and submitted via email before the confirmed due date
.
Key Tools & Resources for Research
Students are encouraged to consult official documentation and beginner-friendly platforms during their research, including:
Cloud Platforms: IBM, Google Cloud, AWS, or Microsoft Azure for data fundamentals
.
AI Resources: Google's Machine Learning Crash Course or OpenAI’s blog for AI concepts
.
Developer Documentation: Postman or RapidAPI for understanding APIs
.
Governance & Careers: World Economic Forum or LinkedIn Learning for ethics and career pathways
.
